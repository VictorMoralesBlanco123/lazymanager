# Privacy Policy for Lazy Manager

**Effective date:** July 6, 2026
**Developer:** Victor Morales Blanco
**App:** Lazy Manager (`io.github.victormoralesblanco123.lazymanagerv21`)

## The short version

Lazy Manager is a break and lunch scheduling tool for retail shift managers. Everything you enter stays on your device, is automatically deleted at the end of each day, and is never transmitted to the developer or sold or shared with anyone. The app has no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no servers.

## Information stored on your device

When you use Lazy Manager, you may enter schedule information such as associates' names, roles, shift times, break and lunch times, and whether an associate is a minor. This information is stored only in the app's private storage on your device. It is never uploaded, synced, backed up to the cloud, or accessible to the developer or any third party.

This data is temporary by design: the app automatically deletes each day's schedule at midnight, and you can remove any individual entry at any time from within the app. Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all of its data. Because no copy ever leaves your device, there is nothing for the developer to retain, recover, or delete on your behalf.

## Photos and text recognition

If you use the "From a photo" feature to add associates from a picture of a posted schedule, the photo is processed entirely on your device using Google's ML Kit text recognition, which ships with the app and works offline. The photo and the text recognized from it are never uploaded by the app. Photos you take through this feature are saved temporarily in the app's private cache on your device and are removed when the cache is cleared or the app is uninstalled. Photos chosen from your gallery are read in place and are not copied anywhere outside your device.

## What the app does not collect

Lazy Manager does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal information. It contains no advertising, no analytics or tracking SDKs added by the developer, no user accounts or sign-in, and it does not access your location, contacts, calendar, microphone, or files beyond the photo you explicitly select or capture.

## Third-party software included in the app

The app includes Google's ML Kit library to perform on-device text recognition. Your images and the text recognized from them stay on your device. However, per Google's documentation, ML Kit and its underlying Google Play services components may occasionally contact Google servers for purposes such as receiving bug fixes and may send Google limited diagnostic information about the library's own performance and reliability (for example, whether recognition succeeded and how long it took). This diagnostic data does not include your schedule content or photos. This library dependency is also the sole reason the app declares network permissions; the app's own code makes no network requests. For details, see [Google's ML Kit terms](https://developers.google.com/ml-kit/terms) and [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

## Permissions

Lazy Manager requests no runtime permissions. Taking a photo is handed off to your device's own camera app, so the app itself never requires camera permission. The network permissions present in the app package exist only because the ML Kit library requires them, as described above.

## Information about other people

Schedules naturally contain other people's names and shift details. That information exists only on your device, under your control, and is deleted automatically each day. As the person entering it, you are responsible for using the app in line with your workplace's policies.

## Children

Lazy Manager is a workplace tool and is not directed at children under 13. It does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

## Security

All data lives inside the app's private, sandboxed storage on your device, protected by Android's application isolation. Device backup of the app's data is disabled, so no copies are created in cloud backups.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this same address with a new effective date. Because the app collects nothing, changes are expected to be rare and minor.
